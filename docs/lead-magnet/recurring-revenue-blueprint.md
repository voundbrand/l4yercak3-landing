## Page 7-8: Section 2 — The Client Packaging Framework

> **PROMPT:** Intro text on why packaging matters, then a 3-tier pricing table (Bronze/Silver/Gold) with example services and price ranges.

### Stop Quoting Custom. Start Selling Packages.

Custom quotes are killing your margins and your time. Every proposal is a negotiation. Every client gets something slightly different. You can't systematize what isn't standardized.

The fix: package your services into 2-3 clear tiers. Clients pick one. You deliver the same thing every time, with minor customization.

**Example: Marketing Agency Tiers**

| | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What's included** | Landing page + contact form + basic CRM setup | Full funnel (landing + thank you + email sequence) + CRM + booking | Everything in Silver + AI agent for lead response + monthly optimization + white-label portal |
| **Delivery time** | 1-2 days | 3-5 days | 5-7 days |
| **Client gets** | A live page collecting leads | A complete lead generation system | A fully managed, AI-powered client acquisition system |
| **Suggested pricing** | €500-1,000 one-time | €1,500-3,000 one-time OR €299-499/mo | €500-999/mo retainer |
| **Your time per client** | 2-3 hours | 4-6 hours | 2-3 hours/month (after initial setup) |
| **Recurring revenue** | No (unless they need hosting/updates) | Optional monthly add-on | Yes — monthly retainer |

**Key principle:** Your Bronze tier should be deployable in under 2 hours. If it takes longer, your system isn't ready. Gold is where the margin lives — because the AI and automation do the ongoing work, not you.

**Adapt this to your niche.** If you serve dentists, the tiers might be: "Online Presence" / "Patient Acquisition System" / "Full Practice Growth Platform." The structure is the same — the naming speaks their language.

## Page 9-10: Section 3 — The 2-Hour Client Onboarding System

> **PROMPT:** Intro explaining why onboarding should take 2 hours not 2 weeks. Then a numbered checklist table with time estimates per step.

### From Signed Contract to Live in 2 Hours

Most agencies take 2-4 weeks to onboard a new client. That's 2-4 weeks of zero revenue from that client, plus hours of back-and-forth emails collecting assets and information.

Here's the system to onboard any SMB client in under 2 hours.

**Before the call (send this form to the client):**

Collect everything upfront with a single intake form:
- Business name, address, phone, email
- Logo (PNG, transparent background)
- Brand colors (or let them pick from 3 options)
- Services offered (list with prices)
- Business hours
- 10 most common customer questions (FAQ)
- Existing website URL (if any)
- Social media links
- "What does a great new customer look like for you?" (one paragraph)

**The 2-hour onboarding session:**

| Step | Time | What You Do |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Select vertical template | 5 min | Pick the closest funnel template to their business type |
| 2. Apply branding | 15 min | Logo, colors, business name, contact info |
| 3. Customize copy | 20 min | Swap placeholder text with their services, FAQ, and value prop |
| 4. Connect domain | 10 min | Point their domain or subdomain to the new funnel |
| 5. Set up CRM | 10 min | Configure contact fields, tags, pipeline stages |
| 6. Connect email | 10 min | Set up confirmation email + 3-step follow-up sequence |
| 7. Connect payments (if applicable) | 10 min | Stripe/payment link for their services |
| 8. Configure booking (if applicable) | 10 min | Available hours, service types, confirmation messages |
| 9. Test everything | 15 min | Submit test form, receive test email, book test appointment |
| 10. Go live + notify client | 5 min | Deploy, send client their portal login, confirm everything works |

**Total: ~110 minutes.**

The key: steps 1-4 only work this fast if you have templates ready. Section 5 of this blueprint gives you 6 vertical templates to start with.

## Page 11-12: Section 4 — The ROI Pitch Script

> **PROMPT:** Intro on why ROI conversations close deals. Then the actual script in a conversational format with speaker labels and quotation marks.

### Close Clients With Their Own Numbers

SMB owners don't care about your tech stack. They don't care about AI. They don't care about automation. They care about one thing: money.

The ROI pitch uses their real numbers to make the decision obvious. You're not selling a service — you're showing them what they're already losing.

**The Script:**

**You:** "Quick question — how many calls or inquiries do you get per week that you can't answer because you're busy with a job?"

**Client:** "I don't know... maybe 10-15?"

**You:** "And what's an average job worth to you?"

**Client:** "Around €300-500."

**You:** "So if even half of those missed calls would have converted — that's 5-7 jobs per week you're not getting. At €400 average, that's roughly €2,000-2,800 per week in lost revenue. Around €8,000-11,000 per month."

*[Pause. Let them do the math.]*

**You:** "What I do is set up a system that captures every single one of those inquiries — even at 11pm on a Saturday. It responds instantly, books the appointment, and sends them a confirmation. You wake up Monday morning with new bookings in your calendar."

**Client:** "What does something like that cost?"

**You:** "€[your price]/month. And if it doesn't pay for itself in the first month, you can cancel anytime."

**Why this works:**
- You never mentioned AI, automation, or technology
- You used THEIR numbers, not yours
- The cost is tiny compared to the loss they just calculated themselves
- "Cancel anytime" removes commitment fear
- You sold an outcome (bookings in the calendar), not a tool

**Adapt per vertical:**
- **Salon:** "How many people DM you asking if you have openings but you respond 3 hours later?"
- **Restaurant:** "How many reservation requests come in after closing that you never see?"
- **Real estate:** "How many listing inquiries come in on weekends when you're showing other properties?"

The question is always the same: **"What are you losing right now by not being available 24/7?"**
