### Day 3

**Post 4: Value Post - The Graveyard**

```
Let's talk about the graveyard.

You know the one. The folder on your computer with:
- That app you started 6 months ago
- The SaaS idea you "validated" but never launched
- The automation you built for yourself but never productized
- The course you started making

Every builder has one. Most pretend they don't.

Here's what I've learned: The graveyard isn't the problem. Starting is good. The problem is WHY things end up there.

Usually it's one of three reasons:
1. You got bored (the idea wasn't compelling enough)
2. You got stuck (technical or business blocker you couldn't solve)
3. You got scared (shipping means people can judge it)

Which one is your biggest graveyard filler? Be honest.
```

### Day 6

**Post 7: Framework Share**

```
The 3-Question Validation Test

Before you build anything, answer these:

1. Who has this problem? (Specific person, not "everyone")
2. How are they solving it today? (There's always a current solution)
3. Would they pay to solve it better? (Not "would they like it" - would they PAY)

If you can't answer all three clearly, you're not ready to build.

If you CAN answer all three, share them below. Let's pressure test.
```

Unpopular opinion: "Validate before you build" is bad advice for most people.

The advice should be: "Build the smallest possible thing, ship it fast, and see if anyone cares."

### Day 11

**Post 12: Story Post**

```
Story time.

First project I ever tried to sell: A booking system for hair salons.

I spent 3 months building it. Beautiful code. Clean UI. All the features.

Tried to sell it for 6 months. Got exactly 0 customers.

Why? I never talked to a hair salon owner before building. I assumed I knew what they needed. I was wrong.

The features I was proud of? They didn't care. The one thing they actually needed? I didn't build it.

Lesson: Build WITH people, not FOR people.

What's your "I built the wrong thing" story?
```
